Expert Second Opinion – Prof Kühn

This case presents a multifocal, node-positive triple negative breast cancer requiring comprehensive multimodal treatment according to current European and international guidelines.

1. Neoadjuvant Chemotherapy (NACT):

  • I strongly endorse initiating treatment with NACT. In TNBC, neoadjuvant therapy improves surgical planning, provides important prognostic information, and allows real-time assessment of nodal response.
  • Recommended regimen: Anthracycline- and taxane-based chemotherapy, with carboplatin strongly considered given the triple negative phenotype.
  • Consider integration of immunotherapy (pembrolizumab) based on PD-L1 expression status, following KEYNOTE-522 protocol.

2. Axillary Management Strategy:

  • If NACT achieves nodal downstaging, I recommend Targeted Axillary Dissection (TAD), a technique validated in current EUBREAST/AXSANA studies, which significantly reduces the morbidity associated with complete axillary clearance.
  • Should lymph nodes remain positive following NACT, proceed with standard axillary lymph node dissection (ALND).

3. Breast Surgery:

  • Due to confirmed multifocal disease spanning three quadrants of the breast, mastectomy remains the only feasible surgical option.
  • Immediate reconstruction may be considered; however, this decision must be carefully weighed against the mandatory requirement for postoperative radiotherapy. A staged reconstruction approach may prove more favorable.

4. Radiation Therapy:

  • Post-mastectomy radiotherapy (PMRT) to the chest wall and regional lymph node regions is clearly indicated given the nodal involvement and tumor size >40mm.

5. Genetic Testing and Targeted Therapy:

  • Expedited BRCA1/2 germline testing is essential for this patient profile.
  • If BRCA-positive, adjuvant PARP inhibitor therapy (olaparib) should be incorporated into the post-operative treatment plan based on OlympiA trial evidence.
  • PD-L1 immunohistochemical testing should guide potential integration of immunotherapy (pembrolizumab) into the neoadjuvant regimen.
Treatment Recommendation Summary

For patient Saima, I recommend the following treatment sequence:

  1. Neoadjuvant chemotherapy (anthracycline/taxane-based with carboplatin ± pembrolizumab depending on PD-L1 status)
  2. Mastectomy following systemic therapy (immediate or delayed reconstruction to be individually considered)
  3. Targeted axillary dissection if nodal response achieved, otherwise complete axillary clearance
  4. Post-mastectomy radiotherapy
  5. Genotype-directed adjuvant therapy (PARP inhibitors if BRCA-positive)

This treatment approach aligns with current European Society for Medical Oncology (ESMO) guidelines and incorporates evidence from ongoing clinical trial research in triple negative breast cancer.

Expert Second Opinion – Prof Shao

Based on the clinical information provided, this is an operable Stage IIb triple negative breast cancer (TNBC) with nodal involvement and multifocal disease.

1. Neoadjuvant Chemotherapy (NACT):

  • I strongly recommend NACT as the initial step. This allows evaluation of chemosensitivity and offers prognostic information, especially in TNBC where pathological complete response (pCR) is strongly linked with improved survival.
  • Standard regimens include anthracycline- and taxane-based chemotherapy, with carboplatin often added in TNBC to enhance pCR rates.

2. Molecular & Genetic Profiling:

  • Comprehensive genomic testing must be expedited. Particular focus on germline BRCA1/2 mutations.
  • If BRCA-mutated, adjuvant PARP inhibitor therapy (e.g., olaparib) should be integrated post-surgery, as supported by OlympiA trial data.
  • PD-L1 testing should also be performed, as PD-L1 positive TNBC may benefit significantly from adding pembrolizumab to the NACT regimen.

3. Surgical Strategy:

  • Following completion of NACT, surgical management is required. Given multifocal disease across three quadrants, breast-conserving surgery is not feasible. I recommend mastectomy.
  • Axillary management should depend on nodal response: sentinel/targeted node dissection if downstaging occurs, or axillary clearance if disease persists.

4. Radiotherapy:

  • Post-mastectomy radiotherapy is standard due to nodal involvement and tumour size.
Conclusion

The optimal pathway for Saima is:

  1. NACT with biomarker-guided integration (carboplatin ± pembrolizumab)
  2. Mastectomy following systemic therapy (with or without immediate reconstruction, depending on radiotherapy planning)
  3. Axillary surgery tailored to response
  4. Post-operative radiotherapy
  5. Targeted adjuvant therapy if BRCA+ (PARP inhibitor)

This plan is in line with best practice and contemporary TNBC trial evidence in Asia and internationally.
